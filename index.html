// Who I Am

Not a Corporate
Guy. A Plant Guy.

I didn't start in a conference room. I started on the floor, welding, running equipment, figuring things out with my hands. That's where I learned what actually matters in an operation, and it's shaped every decision I've made as a leader since.

Most plants don't fail because of bad equipment. They fail because accountability gets soft, standards start to slip, and nobody wants to say anything about it. I've seen it happen. My job is to make sure it doesn't.

I lead people the way I'd want to be led, with honesty, clear expectations, and genuine investment in their growth. When people know where they stand and believe someone's actually in their corner, they show up differently. And when people show up differently, the plant runs differently.

"When people have structure, investment, and the right tools, they rise. And when they rise, the operation rises with them."

I'm not chasing the next title. I'm chasing better operations and better leaders. That's the work.

Running the Plant

Lean, preventive maintenance, KPIs, not because they're buzzwords but because an operation without data is just guessing. I don't guess.

Leading People

Accountability isn't a punishment. It's respect. I hold my team to a standard because I believe they can meet it, and I stay in the fight with them until they do.

Looking Forward

Technology changes what's possible. But the retirement wave coming through this industry is real, and a lot of hard won knowledge is walking out the door with it. We have to get ahead of that.
